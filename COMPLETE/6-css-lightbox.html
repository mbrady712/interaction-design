Mars

Half the mass of Earth, Mars is a rocky planet with a thin atmosphere. Named after the Roman god of War due to its red hue, this red color is caused by iron oxide in the martian soil. Mars has long excited the imagination due to its earth-like features including mountains, volcanos, valleys, and riverbeds that appear to have been caused by flowing water.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with mass one-thousandth of that of the Sun but is two and a half times the mass of all the other planets in the Solar System combined. The planet was known by astronomers of ancient times. The Romans named the planet after Jupiter, the king of the gods.
